Hong Kong

🌃 One of the world's great dining cities
A place where Cantonese cooking reaches its absolute pinnacle — and where a new generation of chefs is quietly rewriting the rulebook.
★ Asia's No.1 Restaurant · 2026

大班樓 · The Chairman

中環威靈頓街198號 The Wellington · ⭐ 1 Michelin Star
🏆 Asia's 50 Best — No. 1, 2026 ⭐ Michelin 1 Star

In March 2026, The Chairman reclaimed its throne as Asia's Best Restaurant. Chef Danny Yip's quietly revolutionary approach rebuilds Cantonese classics from the ground up — no fusion flourishes, no technique for its own sake. Just obsessive sourcing, generational respect for ingredients, and a kitchen that believes the best cooking is almost invisible.

The restaurant is intimate — just 18 tables in a handsome Heritage building. Dress well, book far in advance, and let the kitchen lead.

  • 🦀
    Steamed Fresh Flowery Crab
    The dish that made the restaurant. Flower crab with aged Shaoxing wine, chicken fat & flat rice noodles. Worth a trip to Hong Kong on its own.
  • 🥩
    Chairman-Style Char Siu
    Thick-cut, caramelised, impossibly tender. Not the usual bbq pork — this is a different beast entirely.
  • 🐦
    Aged Pigeon with Five Spices
    Dry-aged pigeon, extraordinary depth. A dish that stays with you.
  • ⚫
    15-Year Aged Tangerine Peel Beef Ball
    Aged tangerine peel elevates premium beef to something almost spiritual.
📍 The Wellington, 198 Wellington St, Central, Hong Kong 🕐 Lunch 12–3PM · Dinner 6–10PM (Closed Sunday) 🌐 thechairman.hk — book 6–8 weeks ahead 👔 Smart casual dress code

City 01 · Shanghai 上海 · November 23–28, 2026

Shanghai

🏙️ Michelin-starred rooftops, legendary neighbourhood tables
From Asia's 100 Best Bars to 30-year-old xiaolongbao institutions — Shanghai rewards those who seek.
🍸 Rooftop Bar · The Shanghai EDITION · 29th Floor

ROOF Bar

199 Nanjing Road East · Open 5:30PM–1AM nightly (Fri/Sat until 2AM)
🏆 Asia's 100 Best Bars 2026 📍 29th Floor · The Shanghai EDITION

Perched on the 29th floor of The Shanghai EDITION, ROOF is where Shanghai gathers as the sun drops. Panoramic windows frame the exact moment the Pudong skyline ignites — Oriental Pearl, Shanghai Tower, the whole electric circus. The cocktail program is serious (expect theatrical punches, aged spirits, impeccable technique), the vibe effortlessly chic. Arrive just before sunset to secure a window table.

Perfect for: arriving-night drinks, celebratory toasts, or a nightcap after Nanjing Road. One of the finest rooftop experiences in Asia.

📍 The Shanghai EDITION, 199 Nanjing Road East, 29F 🕐 5:30PM–1AM nightly (Fri/Sat until 2AM) 🎫 No reservations — first come, first served 💰 Cocktails ~¥120–180
🥟 Michelin-Recommended · ~30 Years · Near The Bund

橋愛來來小籠 · Qiao Ai Lai Lai

336 Yunnan Road, Huangpu · Open 8AM–2PM & 3–8PM · Closed Monday
⭐ MICHELIN Guide Recommended 🌟 Asia's 50 Best Must-Visit 💰 ~¥60–100/person

Three decades of perfecting one thing. Qiao Ai Lai Lai has no ambitions to be anything other than the best xiaolongbao in Shanghai — and many argue it has achieved exactly that. The tiny dining room is perpetually full, the queue legendary. But every person in that line will tell you the same thing: it is worth it.

The crab roe xiaolongbao — bursting with rich crab roe in a gossamer-thin wrapper — is arguably the finest soup dumpling on the planet. Come hungry. Come early. Come prepared to wait.

  • 🥟
    Crab Roe Xiaolongbao 蟹粉小籠包
    The crown jewel. Rich crab roe filling, gossamer wrapper. Order 1–2 portions per person.
  • 🍜
    Crab Roe Noodles 蟹粉麵
    Luxuriant crab roe stirred through hand-pulled noodles. Rarely done this well anywhere.
  • 🥟
    Original Pork Xiaolongbao 鮮肉小籠包
    The benchmark. Perfectly seasoned pork, pristine broth inside.
📍 336 Yunnan Road, Huangpu (near The Bund) 🕐 8AM–2PM · 3–8PM · Closed Monday 🎫 No reservations — arrive before 1PM on weekends ⚠️ Expect 20–45 min queue on weekends
🍽️ Dining District · Multiple Cuisines · Evening

新天地 · Xintiandi

Huangpu District · Open daily until late
🏛️ Shikumen Heritage Architecture 🌃 Vibrant Nightlife & Dining Combined

Shanghai's most atmospheric dining neighbourhood — 1920s Shikumen townhouses converted into excellent restaurants, bars and cafés. The setting is genuinely beautiful: lantern-lit alleys, restored stone archways, warm lighting. Perfect for a family group dinner when everyone wants something different.

Recommendations within Xintiandi: Sir Stanley's for elevated Shanghainese, Kommune for vibrant social dining, Azul for Spanish tapas with a view. Or wander freely — every doorway is worth exploring.

📍 Xintiandi, Huangpu (Metro: South Huangpi Road, Lines 1/10) 🕐 Most places: 11AM–11PM, bars until late 🚇 ~5 min from ROOF Bar by DiDi 💰 Wide range — ¥80–500+/person
City 02 · Shenzhen 深圳 · November 28 – December 2, 2026

Shenzhen

🌆 Modern, ambitious, and full of pleasant surprises
Sea World Shekou is the culinary heart of Shenzhen's best eating — waterfront dining, excellent international restaurants, and some of the best value seafood in China.
🌊 Waterfront Dining · Shekou · Evening Hotspot

海上世界 · Sea World Shekou

Sea World Plaza, Shekou · Open daily until late
🚢 Minghua Ship Landmark · Open-air promenade 🌃 One of Shekou's Best Evening Areas

Sea World Shekou is the social heart of Shenzhen — a vast waterfront complex anchored by the Minghua Ship, permanently moored and converted into a dining and entertainment destination. The plaza comes alive at night with hundreds of restaurants and bars spilling onto the waterfront promenade. A wonderful place for a relaxed family dinner with bay views.

Highlights: Pizzeria Alla Torre (authentic wood-fired Italian with a stunning terrace), premium Chinese seafood (point-and-choose live tanks), Korean BBQ, Japanese izakaya, and the lively deck bars around the Minghua Ship.

  • 🍕
    Pizzeria Alla Torre — Wood-fired Pizza
    Widely considered one of the best pizzas in Guangdong. Authentic Italian, waterfront terrace. Outstanding pasta too.
  • 🦐
    Live Seafood — any Chinese restaurant
    Shekou is known for live seafood markets. Point, choose, have it cooked to order. Exceptional quality for the price.
  • 🍺
    Minghua Deck — Evening Drinks
    The outdoor deck around the Minghua Ship has multiple affordable bars and a genuinely lively atmosphere.
📍 Sea World Plaza, Shekou, Nanshan District 🚇 Metro Line 2 to Sea World Station (Exit A) 🕐 Restaurants: 11AM–11PM · Bars: until 1–2AM 💰 ¥100–400/person depending on venue
🌆 Waterfront Complex · OCT Harbour · Bay Views

歡樂港灣 · OCT Harbour

Nanshan District · Open daily · Great at sunset
🌅 No.5 Family Attraction in Guangdong 🎡 Home of the Bay Glory Ferris Wheel

Shenzhen's most sophisticated waterfront development — a stunning mix of parkland, public art, dining, and entertainment built around a 120,000 sqm harbour. The Bay Glory Ferris Wheel (the third tallest in China) provides a spectacular backdrop, and the lakeside promenade is lined with excellent restaurants and cafés. Perfect for a leisurely dinner as the sun goes down.

Try: The Anpha Seafood Restaurant for premium Cantonese, or grab a table at one of the many Western restaurants overlooking the water. The atmosphere here is unlike anywhere else in Shenzhen.

📍 OCT Harbour, Nanshan District (Metro Line 5 to Linkia Park) 🕐 Open 24 hrs — restaurants 11AM–11PM 🎡 Bay Glory Ferris Wheel: 10:30AM–10PM 💰 ¥120–500+/person

🍽️ Dining Essentials

💳
Payment
Most restaurants in Shanghai and Shenzhen accept WeChat Pay and Alipay. Some smaller neighbourhood spots still prefer cash. In Hong Kong, cash and credit cards are universally accepted.
📱
Reservations
The Chairman: Book 6–8 weeks ahead via thechairman.hk. ROOF Bar: No reservations — arrive at sunset. Most other restaurants: walk in or ask Jade to call ahead.
🍜
Xiaolongbao Etiquette
Let them cool for a moment — the broth inside is searingly hot. Gently lift with chopsticks, poke a small hole to release steam, then eat in one go. Never dunk in vinegar aggressively — it breaks the skin.
🦀
Crab Season
November is peak hairy crab season in Shanghai — order crab roe xiaolongbao at Qiao Ai Lai Lai without hesitation. Hairy crab is traditionally paired with Shaoxing wine and ginger vinegar.
🥢
Tipping Culture
Tipping is not customary in mainland China — service charges are built in. In Hong Kong, 10% is appreciated at upscale restaurants if a service charge isn't already added.
🍺
What to Drink
Ask for Baijai (Tsingtao) with street food. Pair Cantonese dishes with 茉莉花茶 jasmine tea. At The Chairman, let the staff recommend a wine or sake pairing — they're exceptional at matching.
