大班樓 · The Chairman

中環威靈頓街198號 The Wellington · ⭐ 1 Michelin Star
🏆 Asia's 50 Best — No. 1, 2026 ⭐ Michelin 1 Star 🌿 No shark's fin, sea cucumber or bird's nest — eco-friendly by philosophy

Cantonese cuisine has a history of two thousand years. Today it has evolved to become the most popular and widely influential cuisine among the "eight culinary classical styles" of China. The reason for its success boils down to one keyword: "freshness."

The Chairman's aspiration is to continue this legacy. As much as possible, they use locally sourced ingredients, products from local condiment stores, and seafood from local fishermen — sent to the restaurant every morning at 6 AM from Aberdeen Fish Market. Their cooking style is fundamentally built on tradition, bringing out the original flavours of the ingredients rather than showing off. "The most simple food demands the highest level of culinary skills."

A few examples: their razor clams are steamed with aged lemon aged over 10 years. Their smoked baby pigeon is prepared traditionally with 20-day-old pigeons until the bones are tender. Their chilli jam is made with slow-cooked oxtail. Even the beancurd sheets come from the famous local store Shu Kee. The Chairman soy sauce chicken uses herbs more expensive than the chicken itself.

  • 🦀
    Steamed Fresh Flowery Crab
    The dish that made the restaurant famous. Hand-picked daily from Aberdeen Fish Market at 6AM. Worth a trip to Hong Kong on its own.
  • 🍗
    The Chairman Soy Sauce Chicken
    House signature. Herbs used are more expensive than the chicken itself. A quiet masterpiece.
  • 🐦
    Smoked Baby Pigeon with Longjing Tea
    Prepared the traditional way with 20-day-old pigeons until the bones are tender.
  • 🐚
    Razor Clams Steamed with Aged Lemon
    Lemon aged over 10 years. Fragrant, briny, extraordinary.
📍 The Wellington, 198 Wellington St, Central, Hong Kong 🕐 Lunch 12–3PM · Dinner 6–10PM (Closed Sunday) 🌐 thechairman.hk — book 6–8 weeks ahead 👔 Smart casual dress code

新榮記 · Xin Rong Ji

688 Nanjing West Road, Jing'an · ~5 min from The Shanghai EDITION
🏆 No. 1 Chinese Restaurant Brand in the World ⭐ MICHELIN 1 Star · World's 50 Best Discovery 🚶 5 min walk from EDITION

1995 年 · The launch of Xin Rong Ji

Xin Rong Ji is a name that embodies the meaning of "Flourishing Prosperity." On October 1, 1995, the first Xin Rong Ji restaurant opened in Taizhou, the birthplace of the Tiantai School of Buddhism. Rooted in the bounty of the East China Sea, Xin Rong Ji has gradually expanded to Hangzhou, Ningbo, Chengdu, Xi'an, Wuhan, Shenzhen, Suzhou, Jiangxi, Hainan — and internationally to Hong Kong and Tokyo.

Xin Rong Ji adheres to the culinary philosophy of "First Seek the Authentic, Then Achieve the Sublime." Guided by the Three True Fires — genuine ingredients, authentic flavours, and sincere hospitality — they are devoted to revealing the inherent essence of every ingredient.

  • 🐟

    Michelin inspectors' favourite. Delicate xiao long bao with rich crab roe filling.
  • 🦆
    Baked Duck Foie Gras Buns
    A Xin Rong Ji original — luxury meets tradition. Truffle-scented, extraordinary.

陶陶居酒家 · Tao Tao Ju

Guangzhou · Private Room Dining · Open 6:30PM – Midnight
🏆 One of Guangzhou's Oldest & Most Esteemed Cantonese Restaurants 🥟 200+ Dim Sum Varieties 🌿 Lingnan Heritage · Rose Gold & Olive Green Decor

Tao Tao Ju is one of the most authentic dim sum restaurants in Guangzhou — ranked among the very best, if not the best. Named "House of Happiness," this century-old establishment has been delighting visitors since 1880 with a calm and rich dining experience steeped in tradition and flavour.

For tourists, this authentic evening tea restaurant gives you the option to try every facet of Cantonese culinary culture. With over 200 varieties of dim sum, Tao Tao Ju ensures you can try an enormous range — we recommend coming with a large group so you can order more dishes.

You will be seated in a private dining room — decorated in rose gold and olive green, paying homage to Lingnan architectural heritage. Helmed by a seasoned chef from Hong Kong with over three decades of expertise, the dim sum is impeccably crafted.

Operating from 6:30PM until midnight, it is perfect for an evening meal after exploring the city.

  • 🥮
    Nai Huang Bao 奶黃包
    The signature dish. Exquisitely blending milk and egg yolks — rich, silky, extraordinary. A Tao Tao Ju trademark.
  • 🥟
    Traditional Dim Sum Selection
    Over 200 varieties to choose from. Come with a large group and order everything you can.
  • 🍵
    Traditional Afternoon Tea
    Sweet treats and traditional tea service — the Cantonese way.

🍽️ Dining Essentials

💳
Payment
Most restaurants in Shanghai and Shenzhen accept WeChat Pay and Alipay. Some smaller neighbourhood spots still prefer cash. In Hong Kong, cash and credit cards are universally accepted.
📱
Reservations
The Chairman: Book 6–8 weeks ahead via thechairman.hk. ROOF Bar: No reservations — arrive at sunset. Most other restaurants: walk in or ask Jade to call ahead.
🍜
Xiaolongbao Etiquette
Let them cool for a moment — the broth inside is searingly hot. Gently lift with chopsticks, poke a small hole to release steam, then eat in one go. Never dunk in vinegar aggressively — it breaks the skin.
🦀
Crab Season
November is peak hairy crab season in Shanghai — order crab roe xiaolongbao at Qiao Ai Lai Lai without hesitation. Hairy crab is traditionally paired with Shaoxing wine and ginger vinegar.
🥢
Tipping Culture
Tipping is not customary in mainland China — service charges are built in. In Hong Kong, 10% is appreciated at upscale restaurants if a service charge isn't already added.
🍺
What to Drink
Ask for Baijai (Tsingtao) with street food. Pair Cantonese dishes with 茉莉花茶 jasmine tea. At The Chairman, let the staff recommend a wine or sake pairing — they're exceptional at matching.
