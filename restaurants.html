★ Asia's No.1 Restaurant · 2026

大班樓 · The Chairman

中環威靈頓街198號 The Wellington · ⭐ 1 Michelin Star
🏆 Asia's 50 Best — No. 1, 2026 ⭐ Michelin 1 Star 🌿 No shark's fin, sea cucumber or bird's nest — eco-friendly by philosophy

Cantonese cuisine has a history of two thousand years. Today it has evolved to become the most popular and widely influential cuisine among the "eight culinary classical styles" of China. The reason for its success boils down to one keyword: "freshness."

The Chairman's aspiration is to continue this legacy. As much as possible, they use locally sourced ingredients, products from local condiment stores, and seafood from local fishermen — sent to the restaurant every morning at 6 AM from Aberdeen Fish Market. Their cooking style is fundamentally built on tradition, bringing out the original flavours of the ingredients rather than showing off. "The most simple food demands the highest level of culinary skills."

A few examples: their razor clams are steamed with aged lemon aged over 10 years. Their smoked baby pigeon is prepared traditionally with 20-day-old pigeons until the bones are tender. Their chilli jam is made with slow-cooked oxtail. Even the beancurd sheets come from the famous local store Shu Kee. The Chairman soy sauce chicken uses herbs more expensive than the chicken itself.

  • 🦀
    Steamed Fresh Flowery Crab
    The dish that made the restaurant famous. Hand-picked daily from Aberdeen Fish Market at 6AM. Worth a trip to Hong Kong on its own.
  • 🍗
    The Chairman Soy Sauce Chicken
    House signature. Herbs used are more expensive than the chicken itself. A quiet masterpiece.
  • 🐦
    Smoked Baby Pigeon with Longjing Tea
    Prepared the traditional way with 20-day-old pigeons until the bones are tender.
  • 🐚
    Razor Clams Steamed with Aged Lemon
    Lemon aged over 10 years. Fragrant, briny, extraordinary.
📍 The Wellington, 198 Wellington St, Central, Hong Kong 🕐 Lunch 12–3PM · Dinner 6–10PM (Closed Sunday) 🌐 thechairman.hk — book 6–8 weeks ahead 👔 Smart casual dress code

🍸 Rooftop Bar · The Shanghai EDITION · 29th Floor

ROOF Bar

199 Nanjing Road East · Open 5:30PM–1AM nightly (Fri/Sat until 2AM)
🏆 Asia's 100 Best Bars 2026 📍 29th Floor · The Shanghai EDITION

Located on the 29th floor and surrounded by a stunning ivy-covered trellis, ROOF is an open-air lounge area offering guests a comfortable, intimate setting for casual drinks. Featuring one of the most spectacular panoramic views of Pudong from the Bund, the bar serves a range of classic and innovative libations prepared by EDITION's expert mixologist.

Arrive just before sunset to secure a window table — the moment the Pudong skyline ignites is one you'll remember. Perfect for arriving-night drinks, celebratory toasts, or a nightcap after Nanjing Road.

📍 The Shanghai EDITION, 199 Nanjing Road East, 29F 🕐 5:30PM–1AM nightly (Fri/Sat until 2AM) 🎫 No reservations — first come, first served 💰 Cocktails ~¥120–180
⭐ Michelin Star · Est. 1995 · Jade's Pick

新榮記 · Xin Rong Ji

688 Nanjing West Road, Jing'an · ~5 min from The Shanghai EDITION
🏆 No. 1 Chinese Restaurant Brand in the World ⭐ MICHELIN 1 Star · World's 50 Best Discovery 🚶 5 min walk from EDITION

1995 年 · The launch of Xin Rong Ji

Xin Rong Ji is a name that embodies the meaning of "Flourishing Prosperity." On October 1, 1995, the first Xin Rong Ji restaurant opened in Taizhou, the birthplace of the Tiantai School of Buddhism. Rooted in the bounty of the East China Sea, Xin Rong Ji has gradually expanded to Hangzhou, Ningbo, Chengdu, Xi'an, Wuhan, Shenzhen, Suzhou, Jiangxi, Hainan — and internationally to Hong Kong and Tokyo.

Xin Rong Ji adheres to the culinary philosophy of "First Seek the Authentic, Then Achieve the Sublime." Guided by the Three True Fires — genuine ingredients, authentic flavours, and sincere hospitality — they are devoted to revealing the inherent essence of every ingredient.

  • 🐟
    Braised Yellow Croaker 手燒黃魚
    The dish that made the restaurant famous. Wild yellow croaker with handmade rice cakes in a rich, nostalgic Taizhou sauce. Order without hesitation.
  • 🥟
    Pork & Crab Roe Soup Dumplings
    Michelin inspectors' favourite. Delicate xiao long bao with rich crab roe filling.
  • 🦆
    Baked Duck Foie Gras Buns
    A Xin Rong Ji original — luxury meets tradition. Truffle-scented, extraordinary.
📍 688 Nanjing West Road, Jing'an (5 min walk from The Shanghai EDITION) 🕐 Lunch 11:30AM–2PM · Dinner 5:30–10PM 🌐 xinrongji.com · Book via Dianping or phone 💰 ¥500–1,000+/person · Premium but worth every yuan
🥟 Bib Gourmand · Est. ~2005 · Near The Bund

橋愛來來小籠 · Qiao Ai Lai Lai

336 Yunnan Road, Huangpu · Open 8AM–2PM & 3–8PM · Closed Monday
⭐ MICHELIN Bib Gourmand 🌟 Asia's 50 Best Must-Visit 💰 ~¥60–100/person (exceptional value!)

Bib Gourmand: good quality, good value cooking.

This simple little shop tucked away in a bustling alley has been packed with hungry diners ever since it opened its doors 20 years ago. The chefs are always busy in the kitchen making xiao long bao, the crab roe variety being the most popular. Brace yourself for an even bigger crowd in hairy crab season, when wontons, spring rolls and soups are generously loaded with crabmeat and roe. The classic soupy pork buns are another must-try.

  • 🥟
    Crab Roe Xiaolongbao 蟹粉小籠包
    The crown jewel. Rich crab roe filling, gossamer wrapper. Peak season: November. Order 1–2 portions per person.
  • 🥟
    Classic Soupy Pork Buns 鮮肉小籠包
    The benchmark. Perfectly seasoned pork, pristine broth inside.
  • 🥟
    Hairy Crab Season Specials
    Wontons, spring rolls and soups loaded with crabmeat & roe. November is peak — expect the longest queues of the year.
📍 336 Yunnan Road, Huangpu (near The Bund) 🕐 8AM–2PM · 3–8PM · Closed Monday 🎫 No reservations — arrive before 1PM on weekends ⚠️ Expect 20–45 min queue · Peak hairy crab season: November

新天地 · Xintiandi

Huangpu District · Open daily until late
🏛️ Shikumen Heritage Architecture 🌃 Vibrant Nightlife & Dining Combined

Shanghai's most atmospheric dining neighbourhood — 1920s Shikumen townhouses converted into excellent restaurants, bars and cafés. The setting is genuinely beautiful: lantern-lit alleys, restored stone archways, warm lighting. Perfect for a family group dinner when everyone wants something different.

Recommendations within Xintiandi: Sir Stanley's for elevated Shanghainese, Kommune for vibrant social dining, Azul for Spanish tapas with a view. Or wander freely — every doorway is worth exploring.

📍 Xintiandi, Huangpu (Metro: South Huangpi Road, Lines 1/10) 🕐 Most places: 11AM–11PM, bars until late 🚇 ~5 min from ROOF Bar by DiDi 💰 Wide range — ¥80–500+/person

Shenzhen

🥟 Guangzhou · Est. 1880 · Iconic Cantonese Institution

陶陶居酒家 · Tao Tao Ju

Guangzhou · Private Room Dining · Open 6:30PM – Midnight
🏆 One of Guangzhou's Oldest & Most Esteemed Cantonese Restaurants 🥟 200+ Dim Sum Varieties 🌿 Lingnan Heritage · Rose Gold & Olive Green Decor

Tao Tao Ju is one of the most authentic dim sum restaurants in Guangzhou — ranked among the very best, if not the best. Named "House of Happiness," this century-old establishment has been delighting visitors since 1880 with a calm and rich dining experience steeped in tradition and flavour.

For tourists, this authentic evening tea restaurant gives you the option to try every facet of Cantonese culinary culture. With over 200 varieties of dim sum, Tao Tao Ju ensures you can try an enormous range — we recommend coming with a large group so you can order more dishes.

You will be seated in a private dining room — decorated in rose gold and olive green, paying homage to Lingnan architectural heritage. Helmed by a seasoned chef from Hong Kong with over three decades of expertise, the dim sum is impeccably crafted.

Operating from 6:30PM until midnight, it is perfect for an evening meal after exploring the city.

  • 🥮
    Nai Huang Bao 奶黃包
    The signature dish. Exquisitely blending milk and egg yolks — rich, silky, extraordinary. A Tao Tao Ju trademark.
  • 🥟
    Traditional Dim Sum Selection
    Over 200 varieties to choose from. Come with a large group and order everything you can.
  • 🍵
    Traditional Afternoon Tea
    Sweet treats and traditional tea service — the Cantonese way.
📍 Guangzhou, China (Taxi/DiDi from city centre) 🕐 6:30PM – Midnight daily 🚗 Parking available · Air conditioning · Terrace 💰 ¥150–400/person · Private room dining experience
🌊 Waterfront Dining · Shekou · Evening Hotspot

海上世界 · Sea World Shekou

Sea World Plaza, Shekou · Open daily until late
🚢 Minghua Ship Landmark · Open-air promenade 🌃 One of Shekou's Best Evening Areas

Sea World Shekou is the social heart of Shenzhen — a vast waterfront complex anchored by the Minghua Ship, permanently moored and converted into a dining and entertainment destination. The plaza comes alive at night with hundreds of restaurants and bars spilling onto the waterfront promenade. A wonderful place for a relaxed family dinner with bay views.

Highlights: Pizzeria Alla Torre (authentic wood-fired Italian with a stunning terrace), premium Chinese seafood (point-and-choose live tanks), Korean BBQ, Japanese izakaya, and the lively deck bars around the Minghua Ship.

  • 🍕
    Pizzeria Alla Torre — Wood-fired Pizza
    Widely considered one of the best pizzas in Guangdong. Authentic Italian, waterfront terrace. Outstanding pasta too.
  • 🦐
    Live Seafood — any Chinese restaurant
    Shekou is known for live seafood markets. Point, choose, have it cooked to order. Exceptional quality for the price.
  • 🍺
    Minghua Deck — Evening Drinks
    The outdoor deck around the Minghua Ship has multiple affordable bars and a genuinely lively atmosphere.
📍 Sea World Plaza, Shekou, Nanshan District 🚇 Metro Line 2 to Sea World Station (Exit A) 🕐 Restaurants: 11AM–11PM · Bars: until 1–2AM 💰 ¥100–400/person depending on venue
🌆 Waterfront Complex · OCT Harbour · Bay Views

歡樂港灣 · OCT Harbour

Nanshan District · Open daily · Great at sunset
🌅 No.5 Family Attraction in Guangdong 🎡 Home of the Bay Glory Ferris Wheel

Shenzhen's most sophisticated waterfront development — a stunning mix of parkland, public art, dining, and entertainment built around a 120,000 sqm harbour. The Bay Glory Ferris Wheel (the third tallest in China) provides a spectacular backdrop, and the lakeside promenade is lined with excellent restaurants and cafés. Perfect for a leisurely dinner as the sun goes down.

Try: The Anpha Seafood Restaurant for premium Cantonese, or grab a table at one of the many Western restaurants overlooking the water. The atmosphere here is unlike anywhere else in Shenzhen.

📍 OCT Harbour, Nanshan District (Metro Line 5 to Linkia Park) 🕐 Open 24 hrs — restaurants 11AM–11PM 🎡 Bay Glory Ferris Wheel: 10:30AM–10PM 💰 ¥120–500+/person

🍽️ Dining Essentials

💳
Payment
Most restaurants in Shanghai and Shenzhen accept WeChat Pay and Alipay. Some smaller neighbourhood spots still prefer cash. In Hong Kong, cash and credit cards are universally accepted.
📱
Reservations
The Chairman: Book 6–8 weeks ahead via thechairman.hk. ROOF Bar: No reservations — arrive at sunset. Most other restaurants: walk in or ask Jade to call ahead.
🍜
Xiaolongbao Etiquette
Let them cool for a moment — the broth inside is searingly hot. Gently lift with chopsticks, poke a small hole to release steam, then eat in one go. Never dunk in vinegar aggressively — it breaks the skin.
🦀
Crab Season
November is peak hairy crab season in Shanghai — order crab roe xiaolongbao at Qiao Ai Lai Lai without hesitation. Hairy crab is traditionally paired with Shaoxing wine and ginger vinegar.
🥢
Tipping Culture
Tipping is not customary in mainland China — service charges are built in. In Hong Kong, 10% is appreciated at upscale restaurants if a service charge isn't already added.
🍺
What to Drink
Ask for Baijai (Tsingtao) with street food. Pair Cantonese dishes with 茉莉花茶 jasmine tea. At The Chairman, let the staff recommend a wine or sake pairing — they're exceptional at matching.
