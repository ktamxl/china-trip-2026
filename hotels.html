The Shanghai EDITION

199 Nanjing Road East, Huangpu · Adjacent to The Bund
Opened in 2018 under Ian Schrager's legendary EDITION brand, this is Shanghai's most talked-about hotel — and for good reason. Designed by Neri&Hu, it is a masterpiece of restrained luxury: dark wood, warm brass, lantern-lit corners, and a staircase that winds through the lobby like a piece of sculpture. It is simultaneously a sanctuary and the most happening address in the city.
Why we chose it: Prime location on Nanjing Road with The Bund a short walk away. The ROOF Bar (29th floor) is consistently ranked among Asia's 100 Best Bars — perfect for arriving-night drinks with the whole family. A hotel that makes you feel like you're inside the city, not just near it.

One night only · The Ancient Water Town Experience
★★★★★ · Heritage Retreat · Opened 2021

Ahn Luh Zhujiajiao

1 Xianning Road, Zhujiajiao Ancient Town, Qingpu District
This is the experience the trip was built around. Ahn Luh occupies a meticulously restored 600-year-old Qing Dynasty courthouse in the heart of Zhujiajiao Ancient Town — the "Venice of Shanghai." Each of the 17 villas and bungalows is a private sanctuary, furnished with Ming and Qing dynasty antique furniture, Dyson hairdryers, and Diptyque amenities. Step outside your door and you are inside a 1,700-year-old canal town with lantern-lit bridges and stone pathways. It is unlike anything Shanghai has to offer.
Why we chose it: One night in Zhujiajiao is worth a week anywhere else. After three nights in the city, this is where you exhale. Walk the ancient streets at dusk when the day-trippers have gone, eat by candlelight, and fall asleep to the sound of water lapping at old stone bridges.

November 28 – December 2, 2026 · 4 nights
★★★★★ · Hyatt Lifestyle Brand · Bayside Luxury

Andaz Shenzhen Bay

No. 8 Xieliu Middle Road, Nanshan District, Shenzhen
The creative sibling in the Hyatt family — Andaz is a brand built on the idea that luxury should be individual, not formulaic. Shenzhen Bay is its most spectacular expression yet: a sleek tower rising above the bay with views across the water to Hong Kong's Lantau Island. The 19-metre indoor pool is one of the finest in Southern China, the two restaurants (East Room and West Room) are destinations in their own right, and the vibe is sharply contemporary without ever feeling cold.
Why we chose it: The best luxury value in the Greater Bay Area. Connected to Sea World Shekou by waterfront promenade — you can walk from the hotel to dinner at Sea World in 10 minutes. The views of Hong Kong from higher floors are genuinely moving, especially at sunrise.
